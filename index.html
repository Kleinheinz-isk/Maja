Harry Styles

Harry Styles is a British singer, songwriter, and actor born on February 1, 1994. He gained fame as a member of the boy band One Direction and later embarked on a successful solo career.

In 2010, Styles auditioned for the television show "The X Factor," where One Direction was formed. The group quickly rose to international stardom, releasing hit albums like "Up All Night," "Take Me Home," and "Midnight Memories." After the band went on hiatus, Styles pursued a solo career and released his debut self-titled solo album in 2017.

Styles' solo career has been marked by critical acclaim and commercial success, with hits like "Sign of the Times," "Watermelon Sugar," and "Adore You." Besides his musical endeavors, he has also ventured into acting, receiving praise for his roles in films such as "Dunkirk" and "The Eternals."
